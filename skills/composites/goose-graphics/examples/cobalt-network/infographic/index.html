2026 STARTUP GUIDE

5 tips for building a
startup that actually ships.

Five compounding habits, one network. Each tip below is rendered as a node-cluster figure — a way to see how the practice connects to the rest of a healthy founder operating system.

01
VELOCITY

Ship fast, learn faster

Iteration speed beats planning quality. Launch an MVP in weeks, not months — speed of feedback compounds. Founders who shipped weekly were 2.4x more likely to find product-market fit in year one.

02
PLATFORM

Build AI into the core

In 2026, AI is infrastructure, not garnish. Embed it from day one. Retrofit later costs 3x more in time and technical debt; the teams who treated AI as a load-bearing layer pulled away in the first 18 months.

FIG. 02 / PLATFORM

03
TEAM

Hire for leverage, not headcount

Small teams with high-leverage tooling consistently outperform large bloated orgs. One 10x hire beats three average ones. Headcount is a lagging metric; throughput per person is the real signal.

FIG. 03 / TEAM

04
USERS

Obsess over 10 users before 10,000

Deep retention with a few beats shallow acquisition at scale. Talk to your users daily until you can't stand not to. The early relationships become the moat — and the source of every honest insight you'll get.

FIG. 04 / USERS

High-trust ring
05
CAPITAL

Revenue beats runway

Default to revenue-positive as early as possible. Fundraising is a tool, not a strategy. Charge people money — paid users tell the truth in a way free users never will, and the cash buys time without dilution.
