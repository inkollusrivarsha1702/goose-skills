5 Tips for
Building a Startup
in 2026

Five operating principles for founders shipping in a market where AI compounds advantage, capital is patient, and distribution beats novelty.

Operating Principles · § 01–05 Vol. 26 / Issue 04
01

Ship Fast,
Learn Faster

Velocity is the only durable moat. Compress your cycle until shipping daily feels boring, then go faster. Every release is an experiment, every customer touch is a hypothesis. The team that runs the fastest learning loop wins the category, regardless of who started with the better idea.

02

Build AI
Into the Core

In 2026, AI is not a feature, a chatbot, or a sidebar — it is the spine. Architect from the model out, not the UI in. The companies that retrained at the foundation are now compounding at speeds that bolt-on integrations cannot match. Your core competency is your data pipeline.

03

Hire for Leverage,
Not Headcount

Headcount is a liability. Hire operators who clear ten meters of work for every meter of payroll. A team of eight extraordinary people will out-ship a team of forty average ones — and they will burn less. The right hire compounds; the wrong hire taxes every roadmap meeting for a year.

04

Obsess Over 10
Before 10,000

Ten loud, loyal users will outpredict ten thousand quiet ones. Get on the phone, watch the screen, ship the fix before the next call. Your first ten are your real product team — they will tell you what to build long before any analytics dashboard does. Most founders skip this. Don't.

05

Revenue
Beats Runway

A dollar of revenue is a vote. A dollar of runway is a deadline. Optimise for the vote. Charging early forces the conversation that fundraising delays — what is this actually worth, and to whom. Founders who price their product on day one ship sharper, focus harder, and outlast their well-funded peers.
