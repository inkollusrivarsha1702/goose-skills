In Review — Five Lessons

Five Tips,
2026.

A quiet letter to founders shipping into a noisier year, distilled from twenty‑three operators.

No. 01 · Velocity

Ship fast, learn faster.

Median time-to-launch among the founders who broke out: seven days. Iteration speed beats perfect planning. Embarrass yourself early and let the market correct the embarrassment.

No. 02 · AI-Native

Build AI into the core.

Eighty-four percent of breakout startups in 2026 treated AI as infrastructure from day one. Retrofitting later costs roughly three times more in time and technical debt.

No. 03 · Leverage

Hire for leverage,
not headcount.

Small teams with high-leverage tooling consistently outperform bloated orgs. One ten-times hire beats three average ones — and is dramatically easier to manage.

No. 04 · Retention

Obsess over 10 users
before 10,000.

Deep retention with a small cohort outperforms shallow acquisition at scale every time. Talk to your first ten users daily until you can't stand not to.

No. 05 · Revenue

Revenue beats runway.

The median breakout reached five million in ARR before raising a Series A. Default to revenue-positive early — fundraising is a tool, not a strategy.
